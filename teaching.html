Teaching & Supervision

Teaching at University of Manchester

1. Tutorials in Health Data Science (Postgraduate). The module covered how to gather individual pieces of knowledge and form understanding in order to be able to devise an appropriate strategy to improve healthcare. The main topic is how to clinical decision support systems help clinicians in decision-making.

Teaching at Queen Mary University of London

1. Bayesian Decision and Risk Analysis (Postgraduate). The module covered an introduction to information systems, types of information system, uses of Information systems, information systems in e-commerce and e-business, information system design and development, case studies of business information systems, the human factor in information systems, and legal and ethical issues in Information systems.

2. Bayesian Decision and Risk Analysis (Undergraduate). This module provided a framework for articulating and managing the risks inherent in the systems you will develop as a practitioner. It also covered how to build decision-support tools for uncertain problems in a variety of contexts (legal, medical, and safety), but with a special emphasis on software development. It brought a principled approach to bear to analysing and solving uncertain and risky problems.

3. Risk and Decision-Making for Data Science and AI (Postgraduate). This module provided a comprehensive overview of the challenges of risk assessment, prediction and decision-making covering public health and medicine, the law, government strategy, transport safety and consumer protection.

4. Introduction to Business Information Systems (Science and Engineering Foundation Programming). This module covered balances business and technical aspects but adopts a high-level view, aiming for example to explain the purpose and use of databases rather than develop specific skills in database query or design.
